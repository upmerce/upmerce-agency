## Part 1: The OTA Squeeze – The Financial Pain Killing Your Margins

Online Travel Agencies (OTAs) are not your partners; in many cases, they act as silent partners taking a massive cut of your hard work.

In Morocco, **Booking.com** takes an average commission of **15%** (ranging from 10% to 25% depending on your property type and participation in programs like Genius). Crucially, this commission is charged on the *total reservation value*, including taxes and fees. Even worse, if you don't manage your "No-Shows" strictly, you pay commission on guests who never arrived.

For a typical mid-sized Riad or Camp in Mhamid generating **3,000,000 MAD** annually via OTAs, that is **450,000 to 750,000 MAD** vanished from the Drâa Valley every single year.

**Viator (Tripadvisor)** is even more aggressive on tours:

* **Agents:** 8% commission.
* **Suppliers (You):** 20% to 30% commission on direct listings.

**The Impact:** A standard 3-day Sahara trek priced at 1,500 MAD per person loses **300 to 450 MAD per booking**.

### The Local Reality (FNIH Insights)
The *Fédération Nationale de l'Industrie Hôtelière* (FNIH) has raised alarms about these practices. In 2024, they highlighted that delayed payments from OTAs (often 45-60 days) exacerbate cash flow issues, specifically for seasonal businesses in oases like Zagora that struggle during the hot summer months.

> **Key Takeaway:** Audit your contracts immediately. Use **Moroccan Law 31-08** (which bans strict rate parity) to legally offer 10-20% exclusive discounts or perks (like free 4x4 transfers) on your own website.

## Part 2: The Technical Reality – Why Your WordPress Site Fails in the Dunes

The desert is unforgiving, and so is the internet connection. Spotty 3G/4G coverage in Mhamid and Zagora demands speed, yet most agencies sell you heavy, bloated WordPress sites.

### The Coverage Data (2024)
* **Maroc Telecom:** Leads with a 9.1/10 coverage experience in remote areas, but latency hits 100ms.
* **Orange/Inwi:** Coverage drops significantly in the dunes (Erg Chigaga), with download speeds fluctuating between 20-50 Mbps and frequent drops to 3G.

### The "Bounce" Problem
On a stable fiber connection in Marrakech, a WordPress site might load in 3 seconds. On a 3G connection in the desert? It takes an average of **19 seconds**.

**Google Statistic:** If a mobile site takes longer than 3 seconds to load, **40% of users bounce** (leave immediately).

**The Result:** You are paying for ads, but tourists can't even open your page to book.
